Customization is a necessary aspect of any component. CDS actively tries to provide variations that provide beneficial options.

## Figma

### Customizations

Figma allows for instances to be customized. Check with CDS documentation to ensure that the customization would be supported and we do not have guidance against doing so.

Common supported customizations:

- Text
- Icons
- Color

### Variations

If it's not a simple customization, we will often build variants to show the flexibility of a component. Before customizing an instance, check to see if there's any variants that are closer to your needs.

### Limitations

Sometimes, we may not be able to represent all variations of a component in Figma. Please double-check CDS documentation of components to see if that variation is enabled even though it is not possible in Figma. If so, you may break the component to achieve the outcome, but you must work with your engineering team to make sure they know how to implement this variation.

## Check-list

### Discuss Customizations with CDS

You may run into a variation that we do not offer, do not recommend, or it's not entirely clear. If so, please reach out to us either via Office Hours or in [#ask-ui-systems](http://go/ask-cds) to get an answer.

### Accessibility

No matter the customization you are making. You're responsible for:

- Your design meets [WCAG 2.1 (AA)](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG21/) guidelines.
- Your design is ready for localization.
- Your design works across all CDS Scales.

### Include all States

If you're designing something custom, consider if it needs states to be specified. Your engineers will need to know the behavior for if a user can load, press, hover, focus, etc. on a component.
