# Content

Content design is all about language. It covers how we write everything from headlines to error messages in order to build trust, convey information, and increase engagement with our customers.

The guidelines here are a snapshot of our [Content Design Guidelines](https://confluence.coinbase-corp.com/display/UXW/Content+Design), which live separately from CDS (for now) and are updated more frequently. Please refer there for the latest info.

On this page, you’ll find 3 sections.

- [Voice](#voice)
- [Principles](#principles)
- [10 popular tips](#10-popular-tips)

If you’re new to Coinbase design, please read through all of them carefully. If you have questions, reach out to #ask-a-writer on Slack.

## Voice

There are three core principles that guide the Coinbase voice.

- **Be bold:** Show the world what’s possible with crypto to change the status quo.
- **Be relatable:** Make crypto and its benefits tangible to everyone.
- **Be positive:** Celebrate the builder in all of us and focus on opportunities.

You can read more, and see examples, in our [Tone of Voice](https://docs.google.com/document/d/17PS0lCwkuK9AKbrJnWoIiCoL_3g-Sw-J7ebKEo12F9I/edit?usp=sharing) doc.

## Principles

Here are a few principles that will help improve your content design across all of Coinbase’s products. To see all of the principles, check out the [Principles](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1QnxOOjhqUb8ENrGt3hQTTDCDxnCPRyEfkO-U-ftm4uc/edit#heading=h.khm8zxfwakyb) section in the Content Design Guide.

### Clear, concise, compelling, consistent

The 4 C’s provide a foundation for designing content effectively.

**Clear writing** is the fundamental attribute of all good product copy. If your copy is confusing, customers won’t engage with it. They will move on and never come back.

- Tip: use design critiques and user research to get feedback on your copy.

**Concise writing** doesn’t just mean short. It means brief but comprehensive. Concise content provides all the info the customer needs and no more than that.

- Tip: keep your copy blocks at 3 lines or shorter. Always aim for less.

**Compelling writing** is where the art of content design comes in. The best writing engages the audience even if it’s transactional. It propels them effortlessly through a flow or task.

**Consistent writing** is how we scale as a team. This includes everything from capitalization to how we talk about our mission.

If your content design is clear, concise, compelling, and consistent, chances are that you’re on the right track.

### Use everyday language

When you write something that appears in our app, it may be read by millions of customers over the course of several years. So write like a real person, not a computer.

- ✅ Your money is on its way
- ❌ Transfer complete

### Write simple and direct instructions

Our customers want to move quickly and easily through every task.

- ✅ Follow the instructions to send crypto.
- ❌ Consult the in-app documentation for further information and instructions in order to send crypto.

### Use present tense and active voice

Use the present tense whenever possible to give your writing momentum and keep it concise.

- ✅ BTC sent
- ❌ BTC has been sent

### Write for accessibility, inclusivity, and a diverse audience

Coinbase has a diverse customer base and our writing should reflect that. As we grow globally, this will become even more important.

- ✅ “Let’s say Maria wants to buy $50 in Bitcoin.”
- ❌ “Let’s say Maria wants to invest $25,000 in Bitcoin.”

Also, use inclusive language, such as “intermediary” instead of “middleman.” Check out our [tricky words](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1V0Io3_E9pGrbKEjLW-1CaYB4zNqxU5xo831_DdZnA0w/edit?usp=sharing) doc for a list. We plan to expand inclusivity guidelines in the future.

### Show your writing to others

It’s easy to just write some copy, pop it into a PR, and never look at it again. Avoid that temptation. Over the years, it may leave an impression on millions of customers. So, please get feedback by sharing your copy with a designer or posting it to #ask-a-writer on Slack.

## 10 popular tips

Here are 10 guidelines we find ourselves sharing frequently. For all of our guidelines, check out our [Content Design Guidelines](https://confluence.coinbase-corp.com/display/UXW/Content+Design) site.

### 1. Use sentence case

We use sentence case in all of our Retail products, and we hope to update Institutional and Ecosystem to sentence case over time. This includes all headlines, buttons, and labels.

- ✅ Learn more
- ❌ Learn More

### 2. “Continue” instead of “Next”

Use “Continue” in button labels to indicate moving forward in a flow.

### 3. Error messages

Use direct, negative language in alerts. State what is wrong, not how to fix it.

- ✅ “Amount too low” (the problem)
- ❌ “Increase amount” (the solution)

For generic errors, use our [Connection trouble](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1QnxOOjhqUb8ENrGt3hQTTDCDxnCPRyEfkO-U-ftm4uc/edit#heading=h.bqjlx0cnlsjn) error copy.

### 4. Mobile push notifications

Push notifications have 2 parts: the title and the body. For the title, make it direct and concise. If you want to include an emoji, put it at the front of the body text. Here’s a simple example:

Title: Price alert

Body: 📈 Filecoin (FIL) is up 12.48% to $91.82 in the past 20 hours.

### 5. Crypto and cash

We have specific guidelines for how to refer to crypto values.

- ✅ You can receive 5 XTZ for free.
- ❌ You can receive 5 free XTZ.

<br />

- ✅ You must stake 50 XTZ or more to receive this offer.
- ❌ You must stake 50 or more XTZ to receive this offer.

<br />

- ✅ You’ll receive $10 in BTC for referring a friend.
- ❌ You’ll receive $10 BTC for referring a friend.

<br />

- ✅ $10 in BTC
- ❌ $10 of BTC

<br />

- ✅ Bitcoin is an asset
- ✅ Bitcoin is a cryptocurrency
- ❌ Bitcoin is a crypto

Some useful definitions:

- “Cash” refers to traditional currency, such as US dollars
- “Funds” refers to cash, crypto, or a mix of both (it’s very flexible)
- An “asset” refers to a single cryptocurrency

Avoid using “fiat” to refer to cash. You can find [more crypto guidelines](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1QnxOOjhqUb8ENrGt3hQTTDCDxnCPRyEfkO-U-ftm4uc/edit#heading=h.lr9p47s3dbsr) in our Content Design Guide.

### 6. Numbers

In general, use a numeral in all product copy. It’s easier to scan.

- ✅ You have 3 unread messages.
- ❌ You have three unread messages.

When you’re working with a round dollar value, look for opportunities to trim the pennies.

- ✅ You earned $10 in Bitcoin.
- ❌ You earned $10.00 in Bitcoin.

For negative numbers, use the minus sign character − rather than a hyphen -.

- ✅ The price correlation is −30%.
- ❌ The price correlation is -30%.

Read more about [number format](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1QnxOOjhqUb8ENrGt3hQTTDCDxnCPRyEfkO-U-ftm4uc/edit#heading=h.ma7u8p5f8ird) in our Content Design Guidelines.

### 7. Tricky words

Sign in or log in? 2-step verification or two-factor authentication? OK or okay? For these and many others, check our [Tricky Words](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1V0Io3_E9pGrbKEjLW-1CaYB4zNqxU5xo831_DdZnA0w/edit?usp=sharing) doc.

### 8. Hyperlinks

Links should be informative and concise. Avoid “click here” or “here” as links.

- ✅ Read more about [how to buy crypto](http://coinbase.com).
- ❌ Read more about how to buy [crypto](http://coinbase.com).
- ❌ [Click here](http://coinbase.com) to read more about how to buy crypto.
- ❌ Read more about how to buy crypto [here](http://coinbase.com).

We often use trailing links in product because inline links may break when localized. Trailing links should not end in a period.

- ✅ You can earn 5.00% APR with Tezos. [Learn more](http://coinbase.com)
- ❌ You can earn 5.00% APR with Tezos. [Learn more.](http://coinbase.com)

### 9. Contractions

Use contractions, like won’t and haven’t. It makes our copy friendlier, more concise, and easier to read.

However, you can skip a contraction to add emphasis in legal or warning copy. For example, ”will not” is stronger than “won’t.”

Tip: use a smart apostrophe ’ rather than a straight one '. On a Mac, type OPTION + }. Google Docs, Figma, and Slack automatically convert to smart apostrophes.

### 10. Exclamation points

Avoid using them, except in marketing copy or in moments where it’s critical to convey excitement. As Google’s design team puts it: “Say it.” don’t “Say it!”

Never use more than one exclamation point per screen.
