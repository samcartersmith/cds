<TextDisplay2 as="p" spacingVertical={0}>

  the principles of the physical world, conveying speed and gravity. Timing makes incoming and
  outgoing elements feel natural and real.

<div id="easing" />
<TextDisplay2 as="h2">Easing</TextDisplay2>
<TextTitle2 as="p" spacingBottom={0}>
  Easing controls the rate of movement and transitions. Like the physical world, objects should
  accelerate and decelerate, emphasizing friction, gravity, and mass.
</TextTitle2>

  <Video

<TextDisplay2 as="h2">Easing Types</TextDisplay2>
<TextTitle2 as="p" spacingBottom={0}>
  There are four types of easing curves that are used as elements enter, exit, and persist across
  the screen.
</TextTitle2>

<TextTitle1 as="h1" spacingTop={10}>
  Global
</TextTitle1>
<Collection spacingVertical={0}>
  <CollectionItem spacingHorizontal={0} spacingEnd={6}>
    <TextLabel2 as="p">
      Global is the most common type of easing. It’s used for objects that move within a view
      between point A and point B. Objects such as buttons, toggles, and selection controls use
      Global to emphasize the start and end of a transition by accelerating quickly and gradually
      slowing down.
    </TextLabel2>
  </CollectionItem>
  <CollectionItem
    flexGrow={2}
    media={
      <Video
        srcLight="/video/lightmode/02_timing/cds_lm_globalTimingMaster.mp4"

        controls
      />
    }
  />
</Collection>

<TextTitle1 as="h1" spacingTop={10}>
  Enter
</TextTitle1>
<Collection spacingVertical={0}>
  <CollectionItem spacingHorizontal={0} spacingEnd={6}>
    <TextLabel2 as="p">
      Enter is a type of easing used for objects that animate into view. Objects come into frame at
      peak velocity and decelerate afterwards. Enter is used for objects such as modals, trays, and
      notifications.
    </TextLabel2>
  </CollectionItem>
  <CollectionItem
    flexGrow={2}
    media={
      <Video
        srcLight="/video/lightmode/02_timing/cds_lm_enterTimingMaster.mp4"

        controls
      />

  />
</Collection>

<TextTitle1 as="h1" spacingTop={10}>
  Exit
</TextTitle1>
<Collection spacingVertical={0}>
  <CollectionItem spacingHorizontal={0} spacingEnd={6}>
    <TextLabel2 as="p">
      Exit is a type of easing used for objects that leave the view. Objects gradually acclerate
      before leaving the view at peak velocity. Exit is used for objects such as modals, trays, and
      notifications.
    </TextLabel2>
  </CollectionItem>
  <CollectionItem
    flexGrow={2}
    media={
      <Video
        srcLight="/video/lightmode/02_timing/cds_lm_exitTimingMaster.mp4"

  Expressive
</TextTitle1>
<Collection spacingVertical={0}>
  <CollectionItem spacingHorizontal={0} spacingEnd={6}>
    <TextLabel2 as="p">
      Expressive is a stylized version of Global easing. It exaggerates the sense of speed to give
      extra attention to an animation. It’s used for objects that move within a view between point A
      and point B.

<div id="duration" />
<TextDisplay2 as="h2">Duration</TextDisplay2>
<TextTitle2 as="p" spacingBottom={0}>
  Motion duration is based on an object’s traveling distance and size. Objects that transition over
  a large area (in position or scale) use a longer duration versus objects that transition over a
  shorter surface.
</TextTitle2>
