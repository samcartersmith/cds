title: Going beyond CDS
---

Diverging from CDS is a necessary and natural part of the design process. CDS should not limit your ability to solve meaningful problems for our users. However, diverging from CDS is not always the easiest solution and there are some things you should keep in mind before embarking.

## When Is It Appropriate to Go Outside CDS

### No CDS Component Yet

You might encounter moments where there is no appropriate CDS component for your needs. In this situation, reach out to CDS to confirm that a component does not and will not exist by the time you ship. Once confirmed, it is appropriate to design a new component for your needs.

In this case, it would be good to work with CDS to look at contribution opportunities.

### CDS Component Is Too Limiting

If a CDS component does not sufficiently cover the needs for your product, reach out to CDS to let them know. If deemed appropriate by CDS, it is ok to break a CDS component and modify the component for your needs.

In this case, it would be good to work with CDS to look at contribution opportunities.

## Process

### How Do You Go Outside of CDS?

It can be as easy as drawing a brand new component from scratch, using different sizes for text, or detaching an instance of a style or component.

Going outside of CDS means when you hand this off, your engineering team will need to build something from scratch to implement this.

### Things You Should Be Aware Of

Anything designed and built outside of CDS is you and your team's responsibility. CDS will not be responsible for any bugs, accessibility or localization issues, or if any updates are needed. If others want to use this component, you'll need to provide ways for others to adopt the latest design of the component and ensure they're properly using it.

During your process, you may receive pushback from other designers, your manager, and your engineering team. This should be expected, so please prepare reasoning on why this is the most ideal path forward for your product. We recommend working with CDS to make sure CDS is aligned in your thinking (this will help your case!).

Engineering has formal checks and approvals, more formal than design. When you hand off work that is outside of CDS, your engineer will likely become blocked from shipping the design as-is. This can cause significant problems such as your project may be becoming blocked until you have redesigned your work within CDS or have to supply reasoning and have that approved by CDS, your manager, and your engineering team.

## Tell CDS ahead of time

The most important thing is to keep CDS and your team aware of when you're planning to going beyond CDS and make sure that it's approved ahead of time. It's safe to assume going outside of CDS without prior approval will lead to your project becoming blocked.
