This guide is intended to help you get started with Coinbase Design System (CDS) and introduce you to the tools and resources included within it. In the following sections we will walk you through our Figma libraries, the best way to browse our components, things to keep in mind when exploring new design solutions, how to prepare your designs for engineering hand-off, and how to work with CDS if you ever have to move outside of our system.

## When to use Coinbase Design System

As a designer, your primary job is to solve problems for our users. You'll carry design work from conceptual through execution and work with engineering to hand-off.

When you finalize your design and prepare your design for engineering hand-off, you'll be expected to have your design work aligned to the latest CDS standards and guidelines. This will help your engineering team properly implement the design and maintain a consistent and high quality experience for our users. To make this process even easier and more efficient, leverage existing components as much as possible. Please note that it's okay to diverge from our standards if necessary, but we expect sound reasoning on why it is important for the product, business, or our users, and ask that your team and CDS are in agreement for each divergence.

## Tools

At Coinbase, [Figma](https://figma.com) is our default design tool of choice. If you're a designer, you should have received access. If for any reason you do not have access or are missing editor rights, please reach out to IT.

### Libraries

We actively manage 6 libraries within CDS. We use libraries to manage CDS resources in Figma. Libraries all you to insert components from the Asset Tab as well as apply styles to Text, Color, and Elevation/Shadows.

#### CDS Libraries

Depending on what design you're working on, we have a main library for you. Every component and style in these libraries exists in engineering for both Web and Mobile.

Each of these libraries is redundant and contains all versions of CDS with a scale applied (Dense or Normal) and a mode applied (Light or Dark Mode). You can enable one or more of these at any given time depending on your needs.

- **CDS Dense 🌙** – Scale: Dense, Spectrum: Dark
- **CDS Dense 🌞** – Scale: Dense, Spectrum: Light
- **CDS Normal 🌙** – Scale: Normal, Spectrum: Dark
- **CDS Dense 🌞** – Scale: Normal, Spectrum: Light

![CDS Libraries in Figma](/img/designers-guide/cds_libraries.png)

#### CDS Beta 🛠 Library

In addition to our main libraries, we have a library where we placededicated to experimental components and styles — CDS Beta 🛠. Here we’re providing access to components that might miss engineering counterparts, or that we’re still evaluating and seeking feedback on. These styles are subject to change, and may be incomplete or break at any time. Please use discretion when using any of these components or styles.

#### Retail Mobile Design System Library

We are actively transitioning this library to CDS Libraries. If it exists in Retail Mobile Design System, you may still use it, but at some point it will deprecated, and it'll be your responsibility to transition the instance to one of the CDS Libraries.

### Enabling Libraries

While inside a file, click on the top left hamburger / three horizontal line icon. Then click on Libraries. This will give you a list of libraries you have access to. Either search or scroll down until you see CDS Libraries.

![CDS Libraries in Figma](/img/designers-guide/libraries.png)

To enable a library, click on the toggle. Enabling more may be useful for speciality work, but it also introduces the opportunity for errors and mistakes.

![CDS Libraries in Figma](/img/designers-guide/main_libraries.png)

### Templates

To kickoff your designs, you might find it easiest to work off of one of our templates.

#### CDS Templates

Within CDS, we provide templates for basic screen structures for iOS, Android as well as mobile and desktop web.

Simply head over to our [templates in Figma](https://www.figma.com/files/657352101224507447/project/25312820/CDS-Templates) to begin.

![CDS Libraries in Figma](/img/designers-guide/templates.png)

Note: For quick access in the future, we recommend favoriting the CDS Templates folder.
