Happy belated New Years!

Hope Q1 has been treating you all well. New Years often brings change, and 2021 will look different for design systems at Coinbase.

This year is the year of unification for us on Coinbase Design System. The team has been hard at work laying the foundation to build a unified design system. We believe the best way to scale is to have a singular, unified design system that all products regardless of team or platform can draw from. This will allow teams to ship higher quality faster.

For us, we’re thinking about 2021 in a few phases:

First phase (We're here) – will be focused on unification where we bring the best of both mobile and web together to make one superior system.

Second phase – will be focused on refinement where we look at opportunities to improve our system and make it better for products.

Third phase – will be where we get to advance design/engineer workflows, push the limits of quality, and remove barriers for our users.

Here’s a few highlights from January and February so far:

## Spectrum, Palettes, and Theming

![Two iPhones displaying a light theme and dark theme interface](/img/blog/2-26/theming.png)

Spectrum, Palettes, and Theming allow our interfaces to adapt seamlessly to our users’ needs. With these tools, we now have unparalleled control over color for CDS Mobile and Web.

Set Spectrum to dark and turn the lights off of any interface (or in Prime Broker set it to light and turn the lights on). We can tweak how Spectrum is applied to our interface with Palettes, and manage it all with Theming through our ThemeProvider component.

[Learn more about Spectrum, Palettes, and Theming](https://cds.cbhq.net/foundation/color)

## Buttons

![Two buttons angled and zoomed in](/img/blog/2-26/buttons.png)

It’s no surprise that buttons have been a primary focus for the CDS team in Q1, however we are excited to announce that buttons V1 are nearly ready to share!

Brought to you by React Native, we’ll be supporting the same buttons we all know and love, but have extended this preliminary set to support two unique themes and scales. Keep an eye out for some fresh documentation outlining usage and general best practices.

## Text

![A list of type styles from Display 1 on top to Headline on bottom](/img/blog/2-26/typography.png)

We’ve launched a new Text component. With CDS color palette being established, Text components are now tightly coupled with CDS palette to ensure consistency.

The retail mobile app is migrating to the CDS typography and soon we will switch to using CDS Text components fully. On the web, to improve text HTML semantic for accessibility and SEO, developers are required to decide which HTML element the text is shown with through the as prop. We will revisit and evaluate this API decision in Q2.

[Play with the new Text component](https://cds.cbhq.net/components/text)

## Box

![A box floating](/img/blog/2-26/box.png)

The box, the most mysterious component of all our components. Some say it controls backgrounds, while others say borders, and even spacing! Well, all the rumors are true, as the new box component satisfies all of our layout needs, and is currently being migrated into the official consumer React Native project.

[Learn the secrets of the Box!](https://cds.cbhq.net/components/box)

## Documentation (cds.chbhq.net)

![A browser window on CDS documentation](/img/blog/2-26/documentation.png)

Need to learn more about CDS? We’ve compiled everything into one place. We have resources on all our thinking, primitives, and components. We know it can be a drag to read documentation, so we’ve added a Live Editor for you to learn through playing with components and their properties, so you can get a feel for CDS before adding into your code.

Our documentation site is being actively worked on, so stay tuned for frequent updates!

[Take a look!](http://cds.cbhq.net/)

## March Sneak Peak

### Brand + CDS

![Coinbase logo](/img/blog/2-26/logo.png)

Part of creating consistent experiences is having a consistent brand across our interfaces, so we’ve been partnering closely with Coinbase’s Brand team to ensure CDS components are ready for what’s next, so when you leverage CDS components you’ll always get Coinbase’s current brand.

Next month, we’re excited to add a new Logo component, which will provide the ability to quickly add Coinbase's mark to any interface. The Logo component combined with our current offerings [Text](https://cds.cbhq.net/components/text) and [Color](https://cds.cbhq.net/foundation/color) will further help keep interfaces aligned on Coinbase’s Brand.

### Navigation

![A vertical navigation bar for coinbase](/img/blog/2-26/navigation.png)

The growing suite of Coinbase products as well as the growing complexity of each — from our Retail products to our Business products — require us to start thinking about Navigation more holistically, to ensure people can intuitively move between products, easily explore each, and quickly find what they are looking for.

As part of our unification efforts and guided by Retail’s recent navigation changes as well as our React Native library, we will soon be able to provide general guidelines and a set of core navigation components for web, featuring the side bar, navigation bar and tabs.

### Iconography

![Eight sets of icons, light on left, heavy on right. Icons include: Portfolio, Advanced, Simple, Notifications, More, Pay, For You, and Activity](/img/blog/2-26/icons.png)

It’s been a thrilling time to be working on Iconography. Some of the core things we’ve been working on are merging heavy and light, and finessing on the new navigation icons! Also, I hear an iconography guideline is in the works too. All coming very soon.
