# Product Module: Scam prevention

> Apply these rules ONLY when the designer specifies Scam prevention as the product area, or when copy educates users about scams, phishing, account takeover, or protecting credentials.

## Never ask users to share secrets — severity: error

- Do not instruct users to share passwords, full seed or recovery phrases, or 2FA codes with "support" or through unofficial links. Education may describe what scammers ask for; it must not mirror scam patterns as if they were legitimate Coinbase steps.

## False urgency and impersonation — severity: error

- Flag copy that creates false urgency about account deletion, legal action, or immediate transfer of funds unless clearly framed as a hypothetical scam example with visible context (e.g. "Scammers may say…").

## Plain-language education — severity: suggestion

- Prefer short, actionable bullets: how to verify official channels, how to report phishing, and what Coinbase will and will not ask for.

## Cross-module consistency

- Apply the ban on generic "For security reasons" and the specificity rules from skills/04-grammar-and-tone.md and skills/07-errors-and-restrictions.md in any scam-prevention messaging that reads like an error or restriction.
